  • What is Capoeira

    Capoeira is a unique Afro-Brazilian martial art that blends dance, music, song, acrobatics and fight into a game that offers the world a tool and methodology to embrace the highest aspirations of the human spirit: liberty, joy, freedom, and wisdom.
    Capoeira is roughly 500 years old and was developed in Brazil by African slaves. It is the only surviving Martial Art native to the New World. Capoeira is more than just another martial art, as it is an art of expression, play and personal interaction developed in the oppressive atmosphere and environment of slavery of colonial Brazil.

Northern Gold Coast Academy

Mestre Torpedo

Presidente & Fundador

Our Capoeira master is a Rio de Janeiro native who has over 40 years experience in our art. Mestre Torpedo lived and taught Capoeira full time in Manila for 4 years and has since migrated to Australia. Our mestre taught and performed Capoeira in Spain (Madrid, Tenerife, Canary Island), Argentina, Japan, Thailand, Taiwan, Indonesia, Maldives, Cambodia and Philippines. Mestre Torpedo was the 1st Capoeira master to live and teach long term in Thailand and the Philippines, and is a pioneer in Asia. He now lives on the Gold Coast.

Sydney Academy

Mestre Lobo

Our Cebu academy was founded by the first and only Filipino Mestre in the history of Capoeira. A practitioner of Capoeira for 27 years, Lobo was awarded his "Mestre" graduation by Mestre Torpedo on 4 Nov 2023. Mestre Lobo was crowned the Asia Pacific Capoeira Champion by the Superliga Brasileira de Capoeira in 2002. Lobo lived and taught here in Cebu for 14 years. He visits us 5 times a year since moving back to Australia before the pandemic. Mestre Lobo set up academies and has taught Capoeira around Asia and all around Sydney. Our founder taught and lived in Bangkok, Jakarta, Phnom Penh, Kuala Lumpur, Cebu and Manila. Many of those classes continue to this day under his students. He has awarded the official Capoeira rank of “Professor” and "Instrutor" to 11 of his students in Philippines and Australia.

Pro Bono

Our students in Cebu and Australia fund pro bono Capoeira classes at orphanages and foundations in the Philippines. While living in Cebu City, our Sydney head teacher, ContraMestre Lobo, with his students, Monitor Coruja and Professor Rabo de Arraia, began pro bono Capoeira classes at 5 community centres and orphanages. Those classes continue today, taught by the 8 instructors in Cebu, most classes run by Professor Rabo de Arraia. Our orphanage classes & uniforms are financed by our Cebu, Gold Coast, Sydney class fees and our students’ donations.

Follow our Philippines academy's facebook page for pro bono class news


We are proud to continue funding pro bono Capoeira classes in Cebu City, where ContraMestre Lobo's mother grew up. We want to teach at every orphanage in the Philippines, and can reach more through your donations, or just by joining our classes. Capoeira, as a holistic art, can develop children's bodies, motor skills, reflexes, co-ordination, rhythm, musical ability, voices and confidence, all in a joyous atmosphere.
